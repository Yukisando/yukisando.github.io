Echoli


My bachelor focusing on the use of audio, I wanted to create a unique experience never seen before in virtual reality gaming: A virtual reality navigation game based on echolocation, where the player is left in the dark inside a big house hunted by a mysterious ghost-like entity. They must navigate undetected to locate the light-switch. The trick is that the game uses live microphone data. Meaning if you want to be quiet in the game, you have to be quite outside the game. This makes for a uniquely scary and truly immersive experience.

Making this game not only taught me a lot about sound physics in general, but how to implement accurate FFT measurements and fine tune settings to make a enjoyable experience.

DS4_2_Synth

A MAX MSP tool that turns any direct input controller into a fully featured synthesizer and sampler. All wirelessly through UDP packages!

This MAX patch receives and sends UDP packages to a Dualshock4 controller to control a wide range of custom made audio effects plugins and buffers. It allows the user to record and playback samples, switch to 5 different instruments and control everything in an ergonomic design. An enhanced version of this patch is now available, feel free to ask me for any files or source code.

MagicArrow

A Java Minecraft plug-in made to enhance the capabilities of the bow and arrow. Enabling this plug-in will add a block where the arrow lands depending on which block you are standing on. For example, if the player is standing on a Redstone block, a Redstone block will be created at the point of impact allowing the player to connect Redstone circuits from a distance.
This plug-in is initially made for players who like to create puzzle-based levels in Minecraft.

Some examples of effects:

Standing on gold: Teleports the player to arrow's location
Standing on Redstone: Creates a Redstone block at arrow's location
Standing on Lapis: Creates a water block at arrow's location
Standing on Sand: Creates a Sand block at arrow's location
Arrow through Lava: Kills the player if the arrow goes through Lava

And some more...

Tested for multiplayer.

AI swimming competition

As part of a university assignment, where I had to code and test different types of path finding algorithms. Once each algorithm was built (Backtracking, Depth-first, Dijkstra and A*), I decided to have them compete with each other.

I thus created virtual swimming pool where 4 contestants race to reach the end. At each round, waypoints are scattered randomly inside the pool and each contestant is allocated a random starting point.
I then simulate a few hundred rounds and graph the results to see which path algorithm prominently won over the others.
As expected, A* ended up dominating the other 3.

PAKNIGHT

A horror game relying on path-finding, ray casting and state machines.



You as the player must pick up all the coins randomly scattered around a play area in a limited amount of time. Unfortunately for you, enemies are patrolling the area looking for you.

If one of them sees you, he will rush at you while alerting all other enemies about your location.
If they get too close, they will kill you.
If they kill you, you die.
If you die, you lose.
If you lose, you cannot win.
Any entities (you included) that falls of the play area dies.
If time runs out, you lose.

As the player, you can spawn a barrier to protect you from enemies directly in front of you. The number of barrier given at start depends on the level of difficulty. Picking up coins will give you a barriers.

What’s under the hood?
 I have used ray casting to detect if the enemies can “see” the player. The way I did it was:

I cast a ray from the enemies to the player. The range of the ray depends on the difficulty level but is always between 15 and 23 meters. If the ray collides with an object with tag “obstacle”, anything through that will not be considered. This is why you can hide behind a wall or one of your barriers.

Enemies have 4 different states:
 If none of the enemies sees the player, the enemies will go in a “patrol” state following a defined route (collection of waypoint) while avoiding obstacles. (They are looking for you)
If an enemy sees you, it will suspend his patrol state and go in a “chase player” state.
If an enemy sees it will alert the others forcing them to enter a “converge” state.
If no you die, win, or lose, the enemies will go in an “idle” state which will make them move much more slowly and end their patrol on the next waypoint.

The enemies use path-finding to avoid obstacles and navigate:
 Since I have implemented A* in the last coursework, I decided to try unity’s navmeshs system. However, I decided to keep a “waypoint system” so I could have different patrols and have different enemies using them.
The navmeshs once backed on the floor and once all obstacles have been set to dynamic mesh obstacles, and enemies set to navmeshs agents. The enemies will avoid any obstacles on their route.
All that was left for me to do is add the code that sets their destination and speed. Both are changed dynamically at runtime.

Polishing:
 In order to make this more of a game then just a demonstration of an AI, I decided to add...

A title screen:
 Has 4 options and a "How to play?" section.
You may select one of the 3 difficulty levels offered to you as well as a special [beta] level which is a randomly generated difficulty where the number and position of both ghosts is random following a few rules. In this mode, no two levels are the same.
The title screen contains two rendering cameras. A "depth only" camera to render close-by objects (the UI in that case) and another camera that only renders the the skybox and rotates. Giving the feeling that the clouds are moving.

Audio:
 The menu sound track is a piece of music I made and slowed down to 20% speed. It gives a nice uncomfortable feeling that sits well with the "horror" aspect of the game. All the enemies make a hover sound to allow the player to located enemies without seeing them. If an enemy sees you, the all famous detected sound form Metal gear solid will warn you about your lack of stealth.

Lighting:
 I wanted the enemies to be menacing. I thus decided to make them almost transparent with clear bright glowing red eyes that cast a red beam.

Design:
 I wanted to make an environment where the player would get lost easily. The twisted wall both move around and rotate making the coins harder to find and the enemies harder to locate. I added a "death cam" so the screen doesn't simply turn black when it ends.

Idea:
 The idea is to have blend between Pacman, Slender and stealth game.
